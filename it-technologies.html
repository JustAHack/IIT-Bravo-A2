What is Cloud and what does it do?

Cloud is simply a model of computing, it is “the on-demand delivery of IT resources over the internet” (Amazon AWS, no date). Resources include access to servers and networks, storage, development tools and applications (apps) that originally would need to be stored and accessed from privately owned and managed on hard drives and servers (Accenture, no date). Cloud Servers, known as Virtual Servers, are stable and fast and secure. They have all the software they require to run and can be accessed remotely via a cloud computing platform (Probrand, no date). There are two types of cloud computing, deployment models and service categories. Deployment models consist of Public Cloud, Private Cloud, Hybrid Cloud and Multi-cloud. Service categories include, Software as a Service (SaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) (Accenture, no date). Cloud Types

Cloud computing eliminates a large portion of the cost of purchasing hardware and software, the cost of running hardware and reduces number of IT staff required to manage infrastructure. The speed at which substantial amounts of resources can be accessed gives business flexibility and ease of capacity planning. Cloud services are accessed on a global scale allowing an elastic scaling, meaning more or less computing power, storage or bandwidth is delivered as required, furthering that power to save more on cost.

Additionally, a secure network of data centers are regularly upgraded to latest fast and efficient hardware, these data centers hosts the biggest of the cloud computing services eg. Azure, AWS, Google cloud. Another major benefit, also relieving costs and workload, is the productivity of using cloud computing; the need for hardware setup, software patching and many other time-consuming management responsibilities are irradicated. Further, the reliability of cloud computing makes business continuity, data backup and disaster recovery easier and less expensive and this is achievable by mirroring data on the provider's network (Azure, no date).

Moreover, Cloud Computing Security has become its own discipline (BeyondTrust, no date). To help protect data, apps and infrastructure, providers usually offer a broad set of policies, include required technologies and controls to strengthen you overall security (Azure, no date).

What is the likely impact?

The impact of cloud computing has had on the world of IT is tremendous. It has, in some way, impacted on almost every individual alive today. Education, social, health, navigation, shopping, or business are just a few examples. Cloud computing could be considered a revolutionary change to how data is stored, maintained, and made available across the world to any device with internet connection. From smart devices such as a fridge, watch, home entertainment system to a more powerful device such as a laptop or mobile phone, everything has access to the cloud with an internet connection. This has made a large impact on the lives of countless of the worlds' population (Willber, 2020).

Although it is difficult to predict the future of cloud computing, the work of the hyperscalers is likely to bring further ease of access, usage and maintenance to a varied but targeted range of users (Accenture, no date). Implementing the modular software will allow a greater plethora of functionality that will not only enrich business, but also the target audience of their products or services (DataFlare, no date). The impact of cloud computing is worldwide and is almost at a point it defines our lives.

It has impacted how businesses and organizations can manage their systems and data, but also how their customers access services (Willber, 2020). Cloud computing is what allows our homes and lives to be filled with the technologies available today. For businesses, the major beneficial effects are noticed are when comparing cloud computing to traditional computing, with the use of cloud services, businesses no longer need to develop or purchase software or purchase and maintain hardware.

The cloud service providers remotely provide management tasks, relieving the need for staffed or contracted technicians to fix technical problems (Watson, 2016). Additionally, individuals reap just as many benefits, cloud services have enabled the lives we live today. From entertainment, social/communication platforms, real-time access of cloud-based apps, navigation, online shopping, and personal storage are all services provided by cloud computing services.

Further, cloud computing has boosted the access and flexibility for the way education is delivered and the healthcare system has adopted cloud computing technology to manage, store and share patient information, allowing the individual and multiple health care professional to access information (Willber, 2020). Cloud services being adapted by a business or organization is described by Watson, 2016. as causing a shift in the structure of the organization.

The structure would create a significant workload shift leaving individuals with new responsibilities. Positions such as managers and customer relationship managers would be replaced by cloud services which ensure workers have access to all that is needed for their work criteria. It would also replace the jobs with responsibility of software and hardware within an organization (Watson, 2016).
